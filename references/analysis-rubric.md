# Analysis Rubric

Use this file when analyzing a portrait before making hairstyle recommendations.

## 1. Photo quality

Judge:
- Can the face be seen clearly?
- Can the front hairline be seen?
- Can the side bulk of the hair be estimated?
- Is the lighting neutral enough?
- Is the camera angle close enough to face level for hairstyle planning?

Output:
- `usable`
- `usable with caution`
- `not reliable for detailed hair analysis`

If uncertain, continue with conservative advice instead of refusing outright.

## 2. Face and head cues

Describe tendencies, not exact measurements.

Key cues:
- face shape tendency: oval, round, longer, square, heart-leaning, broad midface, fuller lower face
- forehead exposure: low, balanced, open, high-looking
- hairline condition: stable, slightly recessed-looking, unclear from photo
- crown impression: flat, balanced, needs lift
- side silhouette: clean, wide, soft, compact

## 3. Hair cues

Judge from the visible photo only:
- density impression: fuller, balanced, lighter-looking
- texture impression: straight, soft, coarse, slightly wavy
- top behavior: flat, structured, naturally lifted
- side behavior: tidy, bulky, clinging, over-short
- current parting: useful, awkward, unclear

## 4. Current-style diagnosis

Identify:
- what already works
- what makes the hairstyle feel tired, flat, severe, messy, or overly plain

Common issues:
- top too flat
- sides too tight
- fringe too heavy
- parting too harsh
- shape too round
- style too conservative without energy

## 5. Strategy translation

Convert the diagnosis into action:
- increase top volume
- soften the parting
- add light layering
- keep mature structure
- avoid aggressive fades
- avoid trend-heavy texture
- expose a bit more forehead
- keep the silhouette neat but not pasted down

## 6. Ranking lens

When ranking styles, prioritize:
1. identity consistency
2. realism
3. suitability for age impression
4. practicality
5. visual improvement
6. style expression

This ranking mirrors the article's principle: consistency and moderate, convincing edits matter more than showing off the model.
