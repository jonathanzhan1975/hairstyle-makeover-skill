# Hairstyle Library

Use this file to pick and rank hairstyle options for realistic portrait recommendations.

## Strong recommendation families

### Natural business side part

Best for:
- mature and professional portraits
- users who already wear side-parted hair
- cases where modest lift and cleaner structure will help

Strengths:
- high realism
- easy maintenance
- preserves identity well

Risk:
- if made too flat, improvement is too subtle

### Natural 3:7 layered side part

Best for:
- users who need a little more softness than a strict business cut
- portraits that benefit from light top volume

Strengths:
- natural
- forgiving
- easy to style

Risk:
- too much layering can become messy

### Light volumized layered short hair

Best for:
- portraits with top flatness
- users who need more energy without looking trendy

Strengths:
- improves spirit and presence
- keeps age-appropriate realism

Risk:
- too much texture can look forced

### Classic Ivy League short hair

Best for:
- conservative but polished recommendations
- users who suit tidy structure

Strengths:
- intelligent, clean, balanced

Risk:
- can become too formal if over-tightened

## Moderate / style-forward families

### Soft Korean side part

Best for:
- users who want a slightly softer, more styled look

Strengths:
- cleaner silhouette
- subtle modernity

Risk:
- can skew too youthful or over-designed

### Textured short crop

Best for:
- users who want controlled texture

Strengths:
- visible change
- more casual energy

Risk:
- easy to overdo

### Brushed-up short hair

Best for:
- users who benefit from opening the forehead and adding front lift

Strengths:
- stronger top presence

Risk:
- can exaggerate hairline or become too styled

### Short neat crew cut

Best for:
- users who value simplicity over refinement

Strengths:
- practical
- clean

Risk:
- often too severe for users who benefit from more crown structure or side softness

## Ranking guidance

### `perfect`

Use when the style:
- matches existing growth direction
- improves energy without changing identity
- fits age impression and practicality

### `good`

Use when the style:
- clearly works
- improves one or two weaknesses
- carries manageable tradeoffs

### `less ideal`

Use when the style:
- is still plausible
- but drifts away from the strongest fit
- or requires more styling, more trend tolerance, or more facial/head-shape support

## Eight-style board guidance

For a full ranking board, mix:
- 3 or 4 highly practical styles
- 2 or 3 moderate alternatives
- 1 or 2 lower-fit styles that are still believable but weaker

Do not include styles that are obviously absurd for the portrait unless the user explicitly asks for contrast.
